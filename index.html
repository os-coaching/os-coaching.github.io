The three phases

Habits. Build. Independence.

Every client runs the same arc: get consistent, chase one goal properly, then learn to run the whole thing yourself.

  1. Habits

    The three non-negotiables go in first — protein, steps, three sessions — sized to your actual week. No overhaul, no banned foods, no starting Monday with fifteen new rules. We build the floor you can still hit on your worst week, because that floor is what everything else stands on.

  2. Build

    Once the habits hold, you pick the goal — a proper cut or a proper lean bulk — and we commit to it. Training progression and nutrition targets pointed at one outcome, adjusted weekly off your numbers. This is where the visible change happens.

  3. Independence

    The next block is your call — cut, build again, or hold. By now you understand the numbers and why everything is set the way it is, so my job shifts to checking your work rather than doing it for you. The method ends with you not needing it.

Meet the coach

I’ve been the before photo — twice.

I was the skinny kid at school — proper rake. Then college happened, the eating caught up with me, and I went the other way. Hated that even more. So I got myself into a gym.

A bit of muscle showed up, I got a couple of compliments, and honestly thought I was massive. I was hooked. I wanted to be properly strong — so I went all in on powerlifting. Years of chasing bigger squats, benches and deadlifts, and it worked: I got genuinely strong. I also looked like I’d never seen a gym. Strong, but carrying it badly — nobody could tell I lifted.

So I switched to powerbuilding: keep every bit of the strength, build a physique that actually shows it. Seven years of training later, I’m in the best shape of my life.

Here’s the part that matters for you: I built all of it around full-time office jobs in financial services — insurance and mortgage broking, long hours, a commute. No training at 2pm because there was nothing else on. The Echelon Method exists because my progress had to fit around a working week. The same week you’re living now.

Not a genetic freak. Not a full-time influencer. Just a system that survives a real life — mine first, then my clients’.
